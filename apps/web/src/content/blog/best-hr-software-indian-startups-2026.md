If you're running a startup in India with 10 to 200 employees, you've probably hit the point where managing HR on spreadsheets and WhatsApp just doesn't work anymore. Someone's leave request got lost. Payroll took three days. A new hire had no idea what their policies were.

The good news: there's no shortage of HR software in 2026. The bad news: most of it is either built for US companies, priced for enterprises, or so complex your team will never actually use it.

This guide compares the best HR software options available to Indian startups right now — with honest takes on pricing, features, and who each tool is actually for.

---

## What to Look for in HR Software for Indian Startups

Before diving into the list, here's what actually matters for an Indian SMB:

- **Indian payroll compliance** — PF, PT (state-wise), TDS under new/old regime, Rebate u/s 87A
- **INR pricing** — dollar-denominated tools get expensive fast
- **Leave management** — CL, SL, EL, optional holidays, carry-forward
- **Ease of use** — your team isn't HR professionals; the tool must be intuitive
- **Employee self-service** — employees should apply for leave, view payslips, and manage their own profile without emailing HR
- **Hiring support** — for growing teams, an integrated ATS saves enormous time

### 2. Keka HR — Best for mid-size companies (100–500 employees)

**Pricing**: Starts at ₹6,999/month for up to 100 employees

Keka is one of India's most established HR platforms. It covers payroll, attendance, leave, performance, and recruitment. The product is mature, well-supported, and handles complex payroll scenarios well.

**Best for**: Companies with dedicated HR teams who need depth over simplicity.

**Limitations**:
- Expensive for small teams
- Can be complex to configure
- Overkill for a 20-person startup

### 3. Zoho People — Best if you're already on Zoho

**Pricing**: ₹48/employee/month (Essential) to ₹125/employee/month (Enterprise)

Zoho People is a solid HR tool that integrates tightly with the broader Zoho ecosystem (Zoho Books, Zoho Recruit, Zoho CRM). If your startup already uses Zoho products, the integration value is significant.

**Best for**: Teams already invested in the Zoho ecosystem.

**Limitations**:
- Payroll requires Zoho Payroll as a separate add-on
- UI feels dated compared to newer tools
- Support quality varies

### 4. greytHR — Best for payroll-first teams

greytHR is a payroll-focused tool widely used by Indian accountants and payroll teams. It handles Indian statutory compliance well and has a large user base.

### 5. Darwinbox — Best for large enterprises (500+ employees)

### 6. BambooHR — Best for remote-first teams with global ambitions

**Pricing**: ~$6–9/employee/month (USD)

BambooHR is popular with tech startups for its clean UI and strong employee experience. However, it's built for the US market — Indian payroll compliance is not native and requires third-party integrations.

**Best for**: Remote-first startups where most employees are not in India or where Indian payroll is handled separately.

**Limitations**:
- No Indian payroll compliance
- USD pricing is expensive for Indian SMBs
- No integrated ATS at base tier

## What Indian Startups Actually Need (and Usually Ignore)

Most founders shopping for HR software focus on payroll and leave. That's important — but the tools that save the most time are usually the ones nobody talks about:

**Automated onboarding**: When a new hire joins, does your current process involve forwarding 6 documents via email and hoping they read them? Good HR software handles this with checklists, document uploads, and policy acknowledgments.

**Self-service for employees**: Every time an employee has to ask HR "how many leaves do I have?" or "when will my payslip be ready?" — that's time wasted. Self-service portals eliminate these questions entirely.

**Compliance tracking**: Mandatory training, document acknowledgments, PF registrations — these are easy to miss in a growing team. HR software that flags non-compliance before it becomes a problem is worth its weight in gold.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is there any free HR software for Indian startups?**
Yes — JambaHR offers a free tier for up to 10 employees with core HR features including employee directory, leave management, and announcements. greytHR also has a free tier for up to 25 employees focused on payroll.

**Does HR software in India handle PF and TDS automatically?**
The better ones do. JambaHR, Keka, and greytHR all handle PF contributions, state-wise Professional Tax, and TDS calculations natively. BambooHR and other US-origin tools do not.

**What's the cheapest HR software for a 20-person Indian startup?**
JambaHR at ₹500/employee/month on the Growth plan works out to ₹10,000/month for 20 employees — covering leave, documents, reviews, training, and payroll. For payroll-only needs, greytHR's paid plans start lower.

**Do I need separate software for hiring and HR?**
Not anymore. JambaHR includes a full applicant tracking system (JambaHire) at the Business tier — job postings, candidate pipeline, interview scheduling, and offer letters all in one place.
