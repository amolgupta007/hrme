## Why Leave Management in India Is Complicated

Indian leave compliance is more complex than it looks:

**Multiple leave types**: Most Indian companies offer Casual Leave (CL), Sick Leave (SL), and Earned/Privilege Leave (EL/PL) as a minimum. Many also add Maternity Leave (26 weeks under law), Paternity Leave, Bereavement Leave, and optional public holidays.

**State-wise variations**: Leave rules under the Shops and Establishments Act vary by state. Maharashtra, Karnataka, Delhi, and Tamil Nadu all have different requirements for minimum leave days, leave encashment, and carry-forward rules.

**Carry-forward rules**: Earned Leave typically carries forward (up to a cap). Casual Leave usually doesn't. Getting this wrong creates disputes during full and final settlement.

**Leave encashment on exit**: Employees are entitled to encashment of unused Earned Leave when they leave. If you haven't tracked balances accurately, you'll either underpay (legal risk) or overpay.

**LOP (Loss of Pay)**: When an employee takes leave beyond their balance, the excess days must be deducted from salary as LOP. This directly impacts payroll.

Manual tracking makes all of this error-prone. Leave management software makes it automatic.

## What Good Leave Management Software Does

A proper leave management system should:

- Allow employees to apply for leave online — no WhatsApp, no email chains
- Route requests to the right approver automatically
- Show employees their real-time leave balance before they apply
- Notify managers instantly when a request comes in
- Update balances automatically on approval
- Block requests that exceed the available balance
- Handle carry-forward at year-end automatically
- Feed LOP data directly into payroll
- Support optional holidays (employees choose which ones to take)
- Show a team calendar so managers can see who's out when

## Leave Policy Setup: What Indian Companies Typically Configure

Here's a standard leave policy structure for an Indian startup as a starting point:

| Leave Type | Days/Year | Carry Forward | Notes |
|------------|-----------|---------------|-------|
| Casual Leave | 8 | No | Cannot be clubbed with EL |
| Sick Leave | 8 | No | Medical certificate for 3+ days |
| Earned/Privilege Leave | 18 | Yes (up to 30 days) | Encashable on exit |
| Maternity Leave | 182 days | N/A | Mandatory under law (26 weeks) |
| Paternity Leave | 5–15 days | No | No central law; company policy |
| Bereavement Leave | 3–5 days | No | Immediate family only |
| Unpaid Leave | Unlimited | N/A | LOP applied in payroll |

*Note: Minimum leave entitlements vary by state under Shops and Establishments Acts. Check your state's specific rules.*

## How to Set Up Leave Management Without Software (and Why It Fails)

Many companies try to manage leave manually:

**Google Sheet approach**: A shared sheet with columns for employee name, leave type, dates, and balance. Works for 5 people. Breaks at 20. At 50, it's a full-time job.

**Problems**:
- No automated notifications — managers miss requests
- No balance enforcement — employees accidentally overdraw
- Balances get out of sync when someone edits the wrong cell
- Year-end carry-forward calculation is manual and error-prone
- No audit trail when disputes arise

**WhatsApp approach**: Approve on WhatsApp, update the sheet later. The "update the sheet later" part never happens consistently.

The operational cost of manual leave management — manager time, admin time, payroll errors, employee frustration — typically far exceeds the cost of leave management software within the first few months.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is leave management software required by law in India?**
No — there's no law requiring you to use software. But accurate leave records are required under state Shops and Establishments Acts. Software makes compliance far easier than manual records.

**How does leave management software handle LOP (Loss of Pay)?**
Good HR software automatically calculates LOP when an employee's leave request exceeds their balance and feeds this data to payroll. JambaHR's Business tier does this automatically — approved unpaid leaves are deducted from the monthly payroll run.

**Can employees apply for leave on mobile?**
Yes — JambaHR's employee portal is mobile-responsive. Employees can apply for leave, check balances, and view approval status from any device.

**What happens to unused leaves at year-end?**
This depends on your policy configuration. In JambaHR, you set carry-forward rules per policy type — Earned Leave can carry forward up to your defined cap; Casual Leave can be set to lapse. The system handles year-end rollover automatically.

**Can I set different leave policies for different employee groups?**
Most HR software including JambaHR allows you to create multiple leave policies and assign them based on employment type, department, or individual preference — useful for companies with full-time, part-time, and contractor staff.
