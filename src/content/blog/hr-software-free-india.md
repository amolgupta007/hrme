"Free HR software India" is one of the most searched terms by small business owners — and for good reason. When you have 10–20 employees, spending ₹10,000/month on HR software feels hard to justify. You'd rather solve the problem for free and pay later when you've grown.

The reality: there are genuinely free HR tools available in India. But most come with significant limitations. This guide tells you exactly what's free, what's a free trial disguised as "free," and what to expect.

---

## What Does "Free HR Software" Actually Mean?

There are three types of "free" in the HR software market:

1. **Genuinely free forever** — core features available at no cost, with paid upgrades for advanced features (freemium model)
2. **Free trial** — 14 or 30 days free, then you pay. Not actually free.
3. **Free up to X employees** — free below a certain team size, then pricing kicks in

Most Indian SMBs looking for free HR software actually need option 1 or 3 — a product that stays free for a small team with the option to upgrade later.

## Best Free HR Software for Indian Companies

### 1. JambaHR — Free for up to 10 employees

JambaHR's Starter plan is permanently free for teams up to 10 employees. No credit card required, no expiry.

**What you get free:**
- Employee directory with org chart
- Leave management (CL, SL, EL, custom policies)
- Indian holiday calendar
- Announcements / company notices
- Employee self-service portal
- Default leave policies auto-configured on signup

**What requires an upgrade:**
- Documents & acknowledgment tracking (Growth — ₹500/emp/month)
- Performance reviews & OKRs (Growth)
- Training & compliance (Growth)
- Payroll with Indian tax calculations (Business — ₹800/emp/month)
- Full hiring pipeline / ATS (Business)

**Best for**: Startups and very small businesses under 10 employees who need basic HR without paying anything.

### 2. greytHR — Free for up to 25 employees (payroll-focused)

greytHR offers a free plan for up to 25 employees that covers basic payroll, leave, and attendance. It's one of the most widely used payroll tools among Indian accountants.

**What you get free:**
- Basic payroll processing
- Leave management
- Attendance tracking
- Employee self-service (limited)

**Limitations of free plan:**
- No performance reviews
- No document management
- No hiring features
- UI is not modern
- Customer support is limited on the free tier

**Best for**: Very small teams where payroll accuracy is the only priority.

---

### 3. Zoho People — Free for up to 5 users

Zoho People has a permanently free tier for up to 5 users. It covers basic HR features and integrates with other Zoho tools.

**What you get free:**
- Employee database
- Leave management
- Basic self-service

**Limitations:**
- 5-user cap is extremely restrictive
- Payroll requires a separate Zoho Payroll subscription
- Indian compliance features are limited on free tier

**Best for**: Solo founders or micro-teams already using Zoho products.

---

### 4. Factorial HR — Free trial only (not truly free)

Factorial offers a 14-day free trial. After that, pricing starts at around ₹250–400/employee/month. It is **not** a free product — don't be misled by "start free" messaging.

---

### 5. Spreadsheets + Google Forms — Actually free, but not scalable

Let's be honest: many Indian SMBs under 20 employees are still running HR on Google Sheets. It works until it doesn't:

- Leave tracking in a shared sheet — works until two people edit at the same time
- Payroll in Excel — works until the formula breaks or someone changes a cell
- Offer letters via email — works until you hire 5 people in a month
- Employee documents in a Google Drive folder — works until you need to prove someone acknowledged a policy

The real cost of free spreadsheet HR is **founder and admin time**. If HR admin takes 10 hours a month at ₹2,000/hour opportunity cost, that's ₹20,000/month — far more than any HR software costs.

## When to Move from Free to Paid

The free tier is the right place to start. Here's when you know it's time to upgrade:

**Move to a paid plan when:**
- Your team crosses 10 employees and leave management is getting messy
- You're running payroll manually and it's taking more than 2 hours a month
- You're hiring and tracking candidates across 3 email inboxes
- A compliance audit is coming and you have no documentation trail
- Employees are asking about their leave balances more than twice a week

At 15–20 employees, the time savings from paid HR software typically outweigh the cost within the first month.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is there truly free HR software in India with no hidden charges?**
Yes. JambaHR's Starter plan is permanently free for up to 10 employees with no credit card required. greytHR's free tier is also genuinely free for up to 25 employees.

**Can free HR software handle Indian payroll compliance?**
greytHR's free tier handles basic payroll. For full Indian compliance — PF, state-wise Professional Tax, and TDS under the new tax regime — you'll need a paid plan. JambaHR's Business tier handles all of this natively.

**What happens when my team grows beyond the free limit?**
On JambaHR, once you cross 10 employees you'll be prompted to upgrade to a paid plan. Your data is preserved — you just unlock more features and capacity.

**Is open-source HR software a good option for Indian companies?**
Open-source HR tools like OrangeHRM exist but require self-hosting, technical setup, and ongoing maintenance. For a startup without a dedicated IT team, the operational overhead usually outweighs the cost savings compared to a freemium SaaS tool.
